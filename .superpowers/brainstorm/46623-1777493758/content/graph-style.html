What visual style for the graph?

This determines the rendering library and how nodes arrange themselves. ~90 nodes total across 16 sections.

A — Force-Directed (Obsidian style)

Nodes repel, links attract. Organic, explorable. Highly connected pages (like Concepts, Architecture) naturally cluster to the centre. Drag nodes, zoom, pan. Built with D3.js or force-graph library.

Best for: discovering hidden connections and seeing which pages are hubs.

B — Colour-Coded Clusters by Section

Same force-directed physics, but nodes are colour-coded by their wiki section (Concepts = blue, Frameworks = orange, etc.) with faint cluster halos. Cross-section links shown in grey. Easier to orient yourself.

Best for: understanding how sections relate to each other, not just individual pages.

C — Hierarchical / Tree Layout

Nav structure drives the layout: sections at top, pages below. Cross-links (See Also) shown as dashed arcs. Predictable and structured, but a wiki with heavy cross-referencing tends to look cluttered.

Best for: clearly showing the nav hierarchy, less good at surfacing organic connections.
